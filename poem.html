A Poem for Cici

In the vast expanse of time, there stands a woman, unique to me,
A woman of trust, one who sets my heart free.
She differs from others, a rarity indeed,
In her, I find solace, my deepest need.

To the heavens, I proclaim, I desire her, it's true,
Not for her looks, nor her body's hue.
Not for loneliness or a heart left to roam,
But to show her she's worthy, to bring her home.

To reveal to her love's true form, pure and serene,
To make her special, in my eyes, a queen.
To spend our days in laughter and mirth,
Creating memories, defining our worth.

Together we'll journey, through rough and through tough,
To be her refuge, when the seas get rough.
To be her sanctuary, her place of reprieve,
Where she finds solace, in the love we conceive.

For she deserves a love, genuine and grand,
A love so pure, as if crafted by hand.
I confess, I've loved before, only to be hurt,
But now I've found her, my soul's true concert.

If she were to hurt me, where would I turn?
For in her, my heart finds its burn.
I've said it once, and I'll say it again,
"If not you, no one," my heart does explain.

I've pledged to myself, in the name of the Divine,
With her, my love will eternally shine.
And now, as she celebrates another year's grace,
Happy Birthday, dear Cici, in love's embrace.
